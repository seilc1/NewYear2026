# Shared Clue Framework

## Purpose
This document provides a reusable clue structure for Acts 1 to 3.

The clues should allow the group to investigate `The Mercenary` effectively without learning the exact crime too early. The evidence should stay broad in Acts 1 and 2, then become conclusive in Act 3.

## Core Rule
- Act 1 clues establish professional reconnaissance
- Act 2 clues establish tools, impersonation, and operational capability
- Act 3 clues reveal the exact objective and final location

## Act 1 Clue Package
Purpose: prove that `The Mercenary` is studying Bern Old Town and preparing a specific operation.

Recommended clue types:
- a marked city map with 3 to 5 possible target locations circled
- CCTV images showing `The Mercenary` or an associate near more than one important Old Town site
- a witness who reports unusual questions about access, delivery times, or restricted routes
- notes about guard shifts, service entrances, stair routes, or public crowd timing
- a stolen printout of emergency procedures or event logistics

What the players should conclude:
- `The Mercenary` is planning something structured
- several different crimes are still possible

Good multi-use locations for Act 1:
- `Bundesplatz`
- `Zytglogge`
- `Kramgasse`
- `Bern Minster`
- `Mattequartier`

## Act 2 Clue Package
Purpose: prove that `The Mercenary` is assembling the means to execute the plan.

Recommended clue types:
- forged police orders or service credentials
- a stash of radios, scanners, timing devices, or jammers
- tool cases containing lock tools, cable, climbing gear, or cutting tools
- fake uniforms, barriers, transport markings, or official-looking paperwork
- a side character who sold or rented unusual equipment without asking questions

What the players should conclude:
- the crime involves infiltration, deception, sabotage, or controlled access
- several crime options remain plausible, but the field narrows

Good ambiguity rule:
- every Act 2 clue should match at least 2 or 3 crime options

## Act 3 Clue Package
Purpose: reveal the final operational step and identify the exact crime.

Recommended clue types:
- a final route change order naming the true target area
- a helper, insider, or coerced contact tied to the actual location
- a dry run, timed equipment drop, or fake emergency setup at the final site
- a communication intercept that names the real trigger condition
- a witness who saw `The Mercenary` preparing the last prerequisite at the true target

What the players should conclude:
- the exact crime is now identifiable
- the team knows where the showdown will happen

## Shared NPC Roles For Clues
Use recurring witness types so the structure feels coherent across crime variants.

- maintenance worker
- courier or driver
- nervous event coordinator
- low-level technician
- street vendor in the Old Town
- private security employee
- city official with incomplete information

## Shared False Leads
These are useful because they fit several crimes before the reveal.

- forged police paperwork that could support robbery, sabotage, or document theft
- technical gear that could fit either infrastructure attacks or signal equipment
- public-event maps that suggest either blackmail, sabotage, or a crowd diversion
- surveillance of federal-quarter movement that could indicate either espionage or a bank-related operation

## Recommended Deduction Curve
- End of Act 1: the team has 4 to 7 plausible theories
- End of Act 2: the team narrows it to 2 or 3 likely crimes
- End of Act 3: only 1 crime should fit all known evidence

## Optional Structure For Each Act

### Scene 1
The team receives or discovers a new clue.

### Scene 2
They investigate a Bern Old Town location, witness, or department lead.

### Scene 3
They misread, mishandle, or partially understand the evidence in a way that supports the parody tone.

### Scene 4
The act ends with a new fact that escalates urgency and points into the next act.
