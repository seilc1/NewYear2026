# Acts Overview

## Core Structure
The session is built around 4 acts.

- Act 1: reconnaissance
- Act 2: tools and capability
- Act 3: final preparation and reveal
- Act 4: showdown at the real crime location

The goal is that the player group only learns the exact crime after Act 3.

## Shared Mercenary Pattern
`The Mercenary` should follow the same operational pattern in every variant.

1. Study locations, access routes, schedules, and security routines
2. Acquire tools, forged authority, communications gear, and dual-use equipment
3. Set up the final entry window, diversion, helper, or prerequisite event
4. Execute the crime and face the group at the target location

## Act 1: Reconnaissance
Purpose: establish that `The Mercenary` is surveying Bern Old Town and preparing a professional operation.

Typical clues:
- copied maps or route sketches
- stolen or photographed access plans
- witness reports of unusual questions about restricted places
- CCTV sightings near multiple possible targets
- notes about schedules, staff routines, or deliveries

Player conclusion at end of act:
- `The Mercenary` is not acting randomly
- he is selecting or preparing a structured target in Bern Old Town

## Act 2: Tools And Capability
Purpose: show that `The Mercenary` is acquiring the means to execute a plan, while keeping the exact objective unclear.

Typical clues:
- forged police or service credentials
- radios, scanners, timing devices, or jammers
- climbing gear, cutting tools, lock tools, or disguised equipment cases
- barriers, uniforms, marked crates, or fake paperwork

Player conclusion at end of act:
- the crime is serious, planned, and likely requires infiltration, diversion, or impersonation
- several crime options should still remain plausible

## Act 3: Final Preparation And Reveal
Purpose: present the last prerequisite that lets the group deduce the exact crime.

Typical clues:
- a fake deployment order
- a coerced insider or recruited helper
- a timed route change
- a staged diversion
- a dry run at the actual target area

Player conclusion at end of act:
- the exact crime can now be identified
- the team knows where to go for the final confrontation

## Act 4: Final Showdown
Purpose: stop `The Mercenary` during the real crime itself.

Requirements:
- the confrontation takes place at the actual target location
- the clues gathered earlier should matter in the final scene
- public chaos, parody police behavior, and pressure from Harris should all peak here

## Design Rule
Act 1 and Act 2 clues should support multiple crime interpretations.

Examples:
- forged police orders can support the bank robbery, treasury hijack, or document theft
- technical gear can support sabotage, signal work, or infrastructure tampering
- access planning can support either political, sabotage, or money-driven plots

Only Act 3 should lock the answer down.
