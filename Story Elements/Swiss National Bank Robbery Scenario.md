# Swiss National Bank Robbery Scenario

## Overview
This is a full 4-act version of the `Swiss National Bank Robbery` plot.

The structure is built so the players only identify the `Swiss National Bank` as the true target at the end of Act 3.

## Core Villain Plan
`The Mercenary` does not intend to storm the bank by force. Instead, he plans to create a false security emergency near `Bundesplatz`, trigger a police-controlled perimeter response, and use forged orders plus disguised personnel to gain controlled access to the `Swiss National Bank` building while the city focuses on the wrong threat.

## Shared False Impression
For most of the session, the evidence should suggest one of these possibilities:

- a political blackmail attempt at `Bundesplatz`
- a federal document theft in the government quarter
- a treasury or protected-transfer hijack

The players should only realize in Act 3 that all of those signs were supporting a disguised bank robbery.

## Act 1: Reconnaissance

### Goal
Show that `The Mercenary` is surveying the federal quarter and nearby Old Town access routes.

### Scene 1: First Lead From Harris
Harris sends the team to follow up on two early reports:

- CCTV footage of `The Mercenary` near `Bundesplatz`
- a complaint from a street vendor who noticed a stranger timing police patrols and delivery access

Tone beat:
- Harris gives the team mismatched files and insults their reading ability

### Scene 2: Bundesplatz Witnesses
The players speak to people around the federal square.

Suggested witnesses:
- `Bruno Moser` in `Characters/Bruno Moser.md`, a coffee stall owner who saw `The Mercenary` sketching barriers and police positions
- `Sabine Aellig` in `Characters/Sabine Aellig.md`, a cleaner who noticed someone watching emergency vehicle access instead of the main crowd areas
- a security guard who remembers questions about evacuation routes and basement service corridors

### Scene 3: Physical Clue
The team finds a folded city map or copied planning sheet containing marked locations.

Markings should include several places, not just the bank:
- `Bundesplatz`
- the federal quarter service lanes
- a nearby delivery route
- one misleading location such as `Zytglogge` or `Kramgasse`

### Scene 4: Act 1 End Beat
The players recover a stolen emergency-procedure printout that covers federal-quarter closures and police perimeter staging.

### Act 1 Clues
- CCTV sighting in the federal quarter
- witness statements about route timing and police observation
- marked map with multiple plausible targets
- copied emergency response paperwork

### Act 1 False Leads
- looks like `Bundesplatz Blackmail`
- could also support `Federal Document Theft`

### Act 1 Conclusion
`The Mercenary` is preparing a professional operation in the Old Town, but the exact target remains unclear.

## Act 2: Tools And Capability

### Goal
Show that `The Mercenary` is assembling the equipment and impersonation tools needed for a controlled-access crime.

### Scene 1: Equipment Purchase Lead
The team gets a tip from a rental or supply contact who provided unusual gear to a fake police subcontractor.

Suggested supplier NPC:
- `Reto Wenger` in `Characters/Reto Wenger.md`, a chatty equipment renter who remembers renting crowd barriers, radios, reflective jackets, and sealed transport cases to a man with paperwork that looked too official to question

### Scene 2: Hidden Stash
The players locate a temporary stash in a garage, storage room, or delivery alcove.

Contents:
- forged police movement orders
- radios and earpieces
- reflective vests or fake security jackets
- lock tools and cable cutters
- numbered traffic barriers or temporary signage

### Scene 3: Department Confusion
The team checks the paperwork and discovers that multiple departments recognize parts of it as valid-looking.

Tone beat:
- internal police bureaucracy creates confusion because half the forms are almost correct
- Harris becomes furious that the fake paperwork is better organized than his own office

### Scene 4: Act 2 End Beat
A private security employee reports that emergency instructions were informally discussed for a possible federal-square incident, but no one can identify who started the discussion.

Recommended witness:
- `Mirjam Kessler` in `Characters/Mirjam Kessler.md`

### Act 2 Clues
- forged police and security paperwork
- radios and perimeter-control gear
- access and entry tools
- evidence of impersonation rather than brute-force attack

### Act 2 False Leads
- still fits `Bundesplatz Blackmail`
- still fits `Federal Document Theft`
- now also fits `Treasury Transfer Hijack`

### Act 2 Conclusion
The operation clearly depends on deception, controlled movement, and official-looking authority, but the exact objective still has not been revealed.

## Act 3: Final Preparation And Reveal

### Goal
Reveal that the false emergency is meant to open the way for a robbery at the `Swiss National Bank`.

### Scene 1: Intercepted Order
The players intercept or recover a final movement order connected to an emergency perimeter drill near `Bundesplatz`.

Critical detail:
- the perimeter closes normal civilian access
- a narrow internal access corridor remains open for an "authorized financial security transfer"

### Scene 2: Insider Or Witness
The team finds a witness tied directly to the final target.

Recommended witness:
- `Jonas Freiburghaus` in `Characters/Jonas Freiburghaus.md`, an anxious SNB security officer who received valid-looking instructions through police channels and now fears that standard emergency procedure has been altered

The officer confirms:
- access procedures were rerouted
- a fake emergency chain was used
- the instructions would expose a controlled route into the bank during the chaos

### Scene 3: Dry Run Or Setup
The team observes or uncovers the final setup.

Examples:
- fake security barriers pre-positioned near the federal square
- a disguised support van staged along a service route
- two recruited helpers already dressed as support personnel

### Scene 4: Act 3 End Beat
The team now understands the real plan: `The Mercenary` intends to use a false public-security incident to isolate the `Swiss National Bank` and enter it under cover of police procedure.

### Act 3 Clues
- named access corridor tied to the bank
- witness from SNB security
- false emergency perimeter plan
- staged support gear near the real target

### Act 3 Conclusion
The exact crime is finally clear: `Swiss National Bank Robbery`.

## Act 4: Final Showdown

### Goal
Confront `The Mercenary` during the robbery attempt at the bank.

### Scene 1: Perimeter Chaos
The false emergency begins near `Bundesplatz`.

Complications:
- civilians panic for the wrong reasons
- ordinary officers block the wrong streets
- Harris screams contradictory orders over the radio
- Lassard may briefly appear to "encourage" everybody and make things worse

### Scene 2: Realization Too Late
The players discover that the visible disturbance was only the cover. The real operation is already moving through the bank access route.

### Scene 3: Inside The Operation
The group reaches the `Swiss National Bank` building and faces:
- forged-authority helpers
- locked checkpoints or misdirected security doors
- a last internal barrier caused by the fake emergency protocol

### Scene 4: The Final Confrontation
`The Mercenary` is encountered during the robbery attempt.

Recommended confrontation elements:
- he remains calm while everyone else is panicking
- he uses police language better than the actual police
- he tries to leave with secured valuables, a transport case, or sealed currency containers
- the players can win through deduction, courage, or spectacularly incompetent improvisation

### Possible Endings
- clean arrest inside or just outside the bank perimeter
- chase into the federal square after the disguise collapses
- partial success where the loot is secured but `The Mercenary` escapes for later reuse

## Core Clue Chain Summary
- Act 1: surveillance of the federal quarter
- Act 2: forged authority and controlled-access gear
- Act 3: emergency rerouting linked directly to the bank
- Act 4: robbery attempt at the `Swiss National Bank`

## Tone Notes
- Keep the crime real enough to feel urgent
- Let the police side of the response be chaotic, contradictory, and funny
- The comedy should come from procedure, incompetence, and character reactions, not from making the threat itself toothless
