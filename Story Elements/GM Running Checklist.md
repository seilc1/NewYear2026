# GM Running Checklist

## Purpose
This checklist helps run the 4-act structure at the table without revealing the answer too early or starving the group of useful clues.

Use it while preparing and while running the session.

## Before The Session
- choose the real crime from `Crimes/`
- read the selected crime file and any linked detailed scenario file
- prepare the Act 1, Act 2, and Act 3 clue packages
- decide which clues are automatic and which require good investigation
- select 3 to 5 witnesses or NPC contacts for the chosen scenario
- choose 2 or 3 false leads that fit the chosen crime
- identify the final showdown location and likely approach routes
- decide how Harris complicates things in each act
- decide where Lassard appears for comic interference or morale support

## Global Rules While Running
- never require a single clue to solve the case
- give multiple paths to the same conclusion
- let failed rolls create delays, complications, or embarrassment instead of dead ends
- keep Acts 1 and 2 broad enough that more than one crime still makes sense
- make Act 3 the point where the answer becomes clear
- let good player reasoning matter more than perfect procedure
- keep public chaos rising as the day advances toward New Year's Eve

## Act 1 Checklist
Goal: establish reconnaissance and create several plausible theories.

- introduce the first concrete lead quickly after the briefing
- include at least 2 different sources of information
- show that `The Mercenary` has studied routes, access, or timing
- place evidence at more than one Bern Old Town location
- make sure the clues fit multiple possible crimes
- end the act with a clue that proves real planning is underway

By the end of Act 1, the players should know:
- `The Mercenary` has a structured plan
- the target is somewhere in Bern Old Town
- several objectives remain plausible

## Act 2 Checklist
Goal: establish capability, impersonation, and preparation.

- reveal tools, paperwork, or specialist equipment
- show that false authority or disguised access is part of the plan
- include one witness who interacted with a fake official, fake contractor, or intermediary
- keep at least 2 or 3 crime theories alive
- add one comedic complication caused by police bureaucracy or incompetence
- end the act with urgency rising and the players narrowing the field

By the end of Act 2, the players should know:
- the crime needs preparation and controlled access
- deception is central to the plan
- the likely theories have narrowed, but the answer is not final yet

## Act 3 Checklist
Goal: reveal the exact crime and point the group to the finale.

- provide the decisive clue even if earlier investigation went badly
- tie that clue directly to the true target or trigger condition
- include a witness, intercepted order, dry run, or setup element that cannot fit the wrong crimes anymore
- make the players feel they solved it, not that the answer was handed to them randomly
- clearly establish where the showdown will happen
- end with the team moving urgently into Act 4

By the end of Act 3, the players should know:
- the exact crime
- the target location
- the likely method `The Mercenary` will use

## Act 4 Checklist
Goal: deliver the payoff at the real crime location.

- start with active pressure already in motion
- make earlier clues useful in the showdown
- include at least one complication caused by mistaken police response or public confusion
- let `The Mercenary` feel prepared and dangerous
- give the players multiple ways to succeed: deduction, confrontation, improvisation, chase, or containment
- end with arrest, escape, partial success, or costly victory

By the end of Act 4, the players should feel:
- they solved the case
- they earned the final confrontation
- the result came from their actions rather than a scripted cutscene

## Clue Safety Net
Use this if the group stalls.

- have Harris accidentally reveal a useful document while yelling at someone
- let a witness come forward because the public incident has escalated
- let the players find a duplicate copy of an earlier missed clue
- have `The Mercenary` accelerate the plan, creating a new visible trace
- let another department call in with information they failed to process correctly

## Tone Control
- keep the villain competent
- keep the police system chaotic
- let the players be funny without making them powerless
- use parody through character behavior, bad coordination, ego, and bureaucracy
- do not let comedy erase the stakes of the crime itself

## Recommended Table Rhythm
- Act 1: curiosity and confusion
- Act 2: competence mixed with escalating nonsense
- Act 3: realization and urgency
- Act 4: pressure, chaos, and payoff
