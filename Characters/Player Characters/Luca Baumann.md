# Luca Baumann

## Role
Player-character template for a patrol rookie.

## Section
Patrol Division.

## Section Head
`Carey Mahoney` in `../Carey Mahoney.md`

## Function In Story
- Gives the team a street-level officer who can handle direct witness contact and public-facing scenes
- Helps the group read suspicious behavior, keep a scene under control, and notice when somebody's story does not fit what happened on the ground
- Works especially well in crowded Bern locations where the case depends on movement, pressure, and improvisation

## What They Are Good At
- Talking to witnesses without sounding overly official
- Noticing when a route, delivery, or street setup feels wrong
- Keeping nervous civilians focused long enough to get useful information
- Stepping into tense situations before they turn into public chaos

## Why They Ended Up On The B Team
- Still too new to be trusted with an important patrol assignment on New Year's Eve
- Good in the field, but not yet polished enough for the department's preferred front-line units
- Got reassigned because the experienced patrol officers were pulled into higher-priority operations

## How To Play Them
- Play Luca as practical and willing to act, but not reckless
- He is more comfortable talking to people on the street than explaining formal procedure
- He wants to prove that basic patrol work can still crack an important case
- He should come across as steady under pressure, even when he is clearly still learning

## Opening Scene Use
- Luca is the one who looks most ready to leave the room and start working immediately
- Harris can treat him as the sort of rookie who thinks enthusiasm counts as a plan
