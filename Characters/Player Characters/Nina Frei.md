# Nina Frei

## Role
Player-character template for a traffic control rookie.

## Section
Traffic Control.

## Section Head
`Debbie Callahan` in `../Debbie Callahan.md`

## Function In Story
- Gives the team somebody who understands road closures, diversions, barriers, timing windows, and vehicle movement
- Helps the group make sense of reroute orders, emergency perimeters, and escape paths
- Works especially well in crime variants involving transport, false police orders, or manipulated response routes

## What They Are Good At
- Reading maps, reroutes, and vehicle access patterns quickly
- Noticing when a diversion or closure order would create a suspicious opening elsewhere
- Predicting which route somebody would use if they wanted to disappear into Bern traffic
- Keeping track of timing when several moving parts have to line up

## Why They Ended Up On The B Team
- Too junior to be given a trusted traffic-control command post during the holiday rush
- Knows procedure well, but has not yet built the authority to make senior officers listen
- Was available because the experienced traffic teams were already committed to the concert and citywide operations

## How To Play Them
- Play Nina as organized, observant, and slightly more comfortable with plans than with improvisation
- She tends to think in routes, timing, and practical logistics
- She does not have to be rigid, but she should like clear structure and visible order
- She wants to show that understanding movement and access can solve more than just parking problems

## Opening Scene Use
- Nina is likely already studying the handouts, map, or timing details before anybody else speaks
- Harris can treat her as the rookie who will ask the inconveniently correct question about who authorized a route change
