# Timon Keller

## Role
Player-character template for a records and evidence rookie.

## Section
Records and Evidence Administration.

## Section Head
`Larvell Jones` in `../Larvell Jones.md`

## Function In Story
- Gives the team somebody who can spot paperwork errors, missing signatures, inconsistent timestamps, and chain-of-custody problems
- Helps the group challenge forged authority and administrative confusion
- Works especially well in crime variants built around false orders, fake credentials, or manipulated internal procedures

## What They Are Good At
- Noticing when a document is formally plausible but still wrong
- Recognizing when records are incomplete, altered, or missing on purpose
- Keeping evidence organized when the rest of the team gets distracted
- Connecting small administrative inconsistencies into a larger pattern

## Why They Ended Up On The B Team
- Considered competent, but too junior and too office-associated to be taken seriously by operational officers
- Useful at a desk, but not yet trusted outside routine evidence and records work
- Got pushed into the assignment because nobody thought the case would benefit from an administrative rookie anyway

## How To Play Them
- Play Timon as attentive, calm, and more confident with details than with status
- He should not feel timid, but he is used to solving problems by noticing what others missed
- He can be quietly stubborn when paperwork does not add up
- He wants to prove that documents and evidence trails matter as much as dramatic fieldwork

## Opening Scene Use
- Timon is the one most likely to notice that the briefing files are incomplete, mismatched, or badly assembled
- Harris can treat him as the clerk he never wanted in an actual operation
