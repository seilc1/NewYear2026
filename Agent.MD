# Agent.MD - Context and General Advice

This file provides context and general advice for working in the Sylvester.RolePlay repository.

## Repository Overview
This repository contains the Sylvester.RolePlay project, designed to support a storytelling experience for a Roleplaying Session on Sylvester Eve (31.12.2026). As the project evolves, specific guidelines and best practices should be documented here.

## Session Details
- **Date:** 31.12.2026 (Sylvester Eve/New Year's Eve)
- **Setting:** Crime-solving mystery in real Bern, Switzerland
- **Players:** 1 Game Master (you) + 4 additional players
- **Location:** Authentic locations in Bern, Switzerland should be incorporated into the narrative

## Creative Direction
- The whole roleplaying session should be a direct parody of `Police Academy`
- The adventure should use chaotic ensemble humor, exaggerated police personalities, absurd authority structures, and comedic crime-solving
- Scenes should combine mystery progression with misunderstandings, recurring gags, slapstick escalation, and public chaos

## NPC Naming Guidance
- Use very obvious near-reference parody names for NPCs inspired by `Police Academy`
- Prefer loud, memorable names and roles such as strict commanders, oddball officers, chaotic recruits, eccentric specialists, and ridiculous suspects
- Example naming style: `Mahoni`, `Lasserd`, `Tekkelberry`, `Heitauer`, `Hux`, `Hooks`, `Callahan`, `Jonz`
- Adapt parody NPCs into the Bern setting where useful, but keep them clearly recognizable

## Story Rules
- Keep real Bern, Switzerland as the grounding for geography, landmarks, and scene placement
- Use real Bern locations as the backdrop for investigations, interviews, chases, police mishaps, and comic set pieces
- Crime-solving remains the core structure of the session even when scenes become absurd and comedic

## Canonical Opening Setup
- The session opens at 07:00 on 31.12.2026 in a meeting room of the main police department in Bern
- The four player characters have been called in by emergency pager message and do not know each other personally, even though they all belong to the same police department in different sections
- Eric Lassard delivers the cheerful and incompetent opening briefing before handing the practical details over to Thaddeus Harris
- Thaddeus Harris informs the group that the famous criminal `The Mercenary` has entered the city and will likely act soon
- A major concert and an ongoing police operation have tied up the competent officers, leaving the player characters as the only available team
- The initial mission is to identify `The Mercenary`'s objective, prevent him from completing it, and bring him into custody

## Intro Guidance
- Treat the user-provided intro scene as the base version of the opening briefing
- Future scenes, clues, NPC reactions, and escalating incidents should build naturally from that briefing
- Preserve the contrast between Lassard's cheerful incompetence and Harris's hostile pragmatism
- Keep the opening pressure centered on New Year's Eve logistics, the big concert, and the threat of imminent public chaos

## General Guidelines
- Follow consistent coding styles and conventions
- Write clear, meaningful commit messages
- Keep functions focused and single-responsibility
- Write tests for new functionality
- Document public APIs and complex logic
- Organize preparation material into dedicated folders for `Characters`, `Locations`, and `Story Elements`
- Store alternative main-crime options for `The Mercenary` in `Crimes/`

## Repository Structure
- `Characters/` for player characters, NPCs, suspects, and supporting roles
- `Locations/` for real places in Bern, Switzerland used in the session
- `Story Elements/` for clues, timelines, scenes, events, and mystery structure
- `Crimes/` for selectable Bern Old Town crime options and main plot variants
